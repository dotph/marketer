# Verified by dotPH — Pre-Launch Marketing Strategy

**Document type:** Operational playbook for the dotPH marketing team
**Product:** Verified by dotPH — free trust & identity verification for .ph domain holders
**Stage:** Pre-launch (product not yet live)
**Objective:** Get first signups and build demand so businesses *want* and *feel the need* for verification
**Channels:** Email mailers (warm + cold) | Facebook Ads | Organic Facebook
**Budget:** $500–$2,000/month (Facebook Ads)
**Timeline:** 8 weeks

---

## Strategic Approach: Problem-First Demand Creation

**Core principle: Lead with the problem, not the product.**

People don't wake up wanting "verification." They wake up worried about scammers copying their business page, stealing their customers, and damaging their reputation. The entire campaign is built around making people feel the problem first, then presenting Verified by dotPH as the only answer.

### The 3-Phase Arc

| Phase | Weeks | Message | Emotion |
|-------|-------|---------|---------|
| **1. PROBLEM AWARENESS** | 1–3 | "This is happening to businesses like yours." | Fear, recognition |
| **2. SOLUTION REVEAL** | 3–5 | "There's now a way to prove you're real." | Relief, curiosity |
| **3. SIGNUP DRIVE** | 5–8+ | "Get verified before everyone else does." | Urgency, FOMO |

**Why this works:**
- Phase 1 creates the emotional need (you can't sell a painkiller to someone who doesn't feel pain)
- Phase 2 positions dotPH as the authority solving the problem
- Phase 3 leverages social proof and scarcity to convert interest into action

### Hybrid Model: Cold vs. Warm Awareness

Not all audiences need the same Phase 1. The strict "no product mention" approach is designed for cold audiences who haven't felt the problem yet. But two segments are already problem-aware:

1. **Warm audiences** — existing .ph domain holders who already trust dotPH as sender
2. **High-scam-exposure industries** — resorts, online sellers, clinics — where impersonation is already a lived experience, not a hypothetical

For these segments, pure problem education risks feeling like a lecture about something they already know. The hybrid approach:

| Audience | Phase 1 Treatment |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Cold** (broad targeting, no prior dotPH interaction) | Problem-only. No product mention. Build the emotional need from scratch. |
| **Warm** (existing .ph holders, FB page engagers, site visitors) | Problem-led, but names the product. "We're building something to fix this. It's called Verified by dotPH." The Zeigarnik loop stays open, but the destination is named. |
| **High-scam-exposure industries** (hospitality, online sellers) | Same as warm — these audiences have likely experienced impersonation directly. They don't need to be convinced the problem exists. |

**Why this works better than one-size-fits-all:**
- Warm audiences start building brand recognition for "Verified by dotPH" from day 1
- When they hit Phase 2, the product isn't brand-new — it's a callback they've been waiting for
- Cold audiences still get the full emotional arc — no shortcuts for people who haven't felt the pain yet

### SECTION 5: FAQ

**Q: Why do I need this if I already have a .ph domain?**
A: A domain proves you registered the name. Verification proves you are the real person or business behind it. It's the difference between owning a storefront and having your ID checked at the door.

**Q: How is this different from SSL?**
A: SSL encrypts the connection between your website and your visitor. It secures the data in transit. Verified by dotPH secures your *identity* — it proves that the person behind the domain is who they claim to be.

**Q: Will this stop scams?**
A: No single tool stops all scams. But Verified by dotPH makes it much easier for your customers to tell what's real. When they can check your verification page before paying, they can avoid the fake pages.

**Q: How much does it cost?**
A: It's free for all .ph domain holders.

**Q: How long does verification take?**
A: Submit your documents through the portal. Review time depends on volume, but we'll notify you as soon as your verification is approved.

**Q: What documents do I need?**
A: A government-issued ID (for individuals) and/or business registration documents (for companies). The exact requirements depend on your verification type.

## Deliverable 6: Cold Outreach Strategy

> For reaching businesses outside the existing .ph domain holder database — new prospects who may or may not own .ph domains.

### List Building Sources

| Source | How to Find Contacts | Quality | Volume |
|--------|---------------------|---------|--------|
| **DTI Business Registry** | Registered businesses with online presence | High | Medium |
| **Philippine Chamber of Commerce** | Member directories by city/industry | High | Medium |
| **Industry associations** | PHILTOA (tourism), HRAP (hotels), restaurant associations | Very high | Low |
| **Facebook business pages** | Search by industry + location, extract public contact info | Medium | High |
| **Google Maps/Business** | Businesses with websites, especially .ph domains | High | High |
| **E-commerce platforms** | Shopee/Lazada sellers with their own websites | Medium | High |
| **Event/expo exhibitors** | Business expo attendee/exhibitor lists | High | Low |

**Priority order:** Start with industry associations and Google Maps (highest quality + relevance), then expand to broader sources.

### Personalization Variables

| Variable | Example | Where to Source |
|----------|---------|-----------------|
| **Industry** | "As a resort owner..." | List segmentation |
| **Business name** | "We noticed [Name] has a strong online presence" | Scraping / manual |
| **Specific pain** | Resorts: fake bookings, F&B: fake delivery pages | Industry mapping |
| **Location** | "Businesses in Cebu are signing up" | Contact data |

### Sending Strategy

- **Volume:** 50-100 emails/day (avoid spam triggers)
- **Timing:** Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM PHT (highest open rates)
- **Sender:** A real person (e.g., "Maria from dotPH"), not noreply@
- **Reply handling:** Monitored inbox. Interested → fast-track to signup. Questions → reply within 24 hours.
- **Warm-up:** New sending domain needs 2 weeks of warm-up before scaling
- **Compliance:** Include unsubscribe link. Honor opt-outs immediately.

## Psychology Principles — Master Reference

| Principle | Where Applied | How |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| **Loss Aversion** | Landing page, emails, ads | "Scammers are profiting from your brand" — losing revenue is 2x more motivating than gaining trust |
| **Zero-Price Effect** | Everywhere | FREE removes all friction. It's psychologically different from even $1. Emphasized in every CTA. |
| **Authority Bias** | Landing page, emails, ads | "By the official .ph domain registry" — instant credibility that no competitor can match |
| **Mere Exposure Effect** | Multi-channel strategy | 7+ touchpoints across email + FB + organic before asking for signup. Familiarity breeds trust. |
| **Mimetic Desire** | Email 3-4, ads, organic posts | "Your competitors are getting verified" — desire is socially contagious |
| **Zeigarnik Effect** | Warm Email 1, Cold Email 2 | Open loops ("we'll show you what we're doing about it") create tension that drives opens |
| **Commitment & Consistency** | Signup flow | Small commitment (signup) → deeper engagement when product launches |
| **Social Proof** | Landing page, emails, organic | Display signup counter, industry-specific adoption, early adopter spotlights |
| **Scarcity Framing** | Week 4+ emails, ads | "First batch" / "Verification opens soon" — urgency without being dishonest |
| **Activation Energy** | Landing page, emails | 3-step process reduces perceived effort. Simple submission feels manageable. |
| **Contrast Effect** | Ads, cold emails | Before (unverified, customers hesitate) vs. After (verified, customers trust) |
| **Bandwagon Effect** | Signup milestones, organic | "[X] businesses signed up this week" — popularity signals safety |

```
Facebook Ads (awareness) → Makes problem feel real → Primes for email
         |

         |
Facebook Ads (retarget) → Solution explainer → Drives to landing page
         |
```

Someone sees the problem on Facebook, gets an email about it, sees the solution on Facebook again, reads an organic post from a verified business — and by the time they hit the landing page, they're already convinced.
