All creative direction below is designed for these tools:

|----------|-------|---------|

| **Music** | Licensed stock, AI-generated stems | Background tracks, sound design |

### AI Production Constraints (Apply to All)

- No consistent human faces across scenes (AI characters shift between frames)
- No lip-synced dialogue — use voiceover with b-roll or text-on-screen
- No phone-shot / UGC style — AI looks too polished for "authentic" formats
- No brand-specific real locations — use stylized or abstract environments
- Use silhouettes, hands-only, or avoid human faces when people are needed
- Text animation and kinetic typography are the strongest tools for this campaign

# CREATIVE DIRECTION: AD 1 — "423% Surge"

## Creative Summary

A 15-second motion graphic that hits like a data punch. Opens with the number "423%" exploding onto a dark screen, then builds context through rapid stat reveals. The emotional arc is shock to alarm — the viewer should feel the scale of the problem before they even read the caption. No product mention, no logo. Pure problem education designed for the Awareness phase.

This works for this audience because Philippine business owners respond to hard data — English-language stats signal authority and credibility. The large number format is inherently scroll-stopping on mobile.

## Specifications

- **Duration:** 15 seconds
- **Aspect Ratio:** 1:1 (1080x1080) for feed, 9:16 (1080x1920) for Stories/Reels
- **Platform:** Facebook Feed (primary), Instagram Feed, Stories
- **Production:** AI-generated motion graphics + kinetic typography
- **Objective:** Engagement / Video Views

## Music & Sound

- **Track description:** Dark electronic ambient. Low sub-bass hum. No melody — tension only.
- **Sound design:** Bass impact on "423%" reveal. Subtle digital glitch texture on stat transitions. No voiceover.
- **Music arc:** Silence → bass hit (0:00) → sustained low tension (0:03-0:12) → fade to silence on end card (0:12-0:15)

## Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: "The Number" (0:00–0:04)

- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Visual:** Black screen. "423%" slams in from center with a scale-up impact animation. White text, bold sans-serif. Slight screen shake on impact. Subtle particle dispersion around the number.
- **Text/VO:** "423%" — no other text yet. Let the number land.
- **Transition:** Number holds for 2 beats, then shrinks slightly upward to make room for context text.
- **Mood:** Impact. Alarm.
- **Technical:** Scale-up from 0% to 110% then settle to 100%. Motion blur on entry. Bass-synced shake.

### Scene 2: "The Context" (0:04–0:08)

- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Visual:** "423%" stays top-center (smaller). Below it, text types out character by character: "increase in phishing websites impersonating PH businesses." Dark background, white text. Subtle scan-line texture.
- **Text/VO:** "increase in phishing websites impersonating PH businesses."
- **Transition:** Hard cut to black (0.2 sec gap) before next scene.
- **Mood:** Mounting alarm. The number now has meaning.
- **Technical:** Typewriter effect at 40 chars/sec. Monospace font for the typing, then morph to clean sans-serif on completion.

### Scene 3: "The Scale" (0:08–0:12)

- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Visual:** Black screen. Stats appear one per second, stacking vertically with subtle fade-in from left:
  - "731 → 3,824 fake sites. One year."
  - "37% more fake brand profiles."
- **Text/VO:** Stats only. No narration.
- **Transition:** All text fades to 30% opacity, then final line appears at full brightness.
- **Mood:** Escalation. Each stat compounds the alarm.
- **Technical:** Stagger animation: each line enters 1 second after the previous. Fade-in from left with 10px travel distance.

### Scene 4: "The Question" (0:12–0:15)

- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Visual:** Clean black background. Single line of white text fades in center-screen: "Is someone copying YOUR business right now?" Source citation in small text, bottom-right corner: "Check Point, 2025"
- **Text/VO:** End card text only.
- **Transition:** Holds to end. No fade-out (Facebook loops).
- **Mood:** Direct confrontation. Personal.
- **Technical:** Simple fade-in (0.5 sec). Hold. No animation on the end card — stillness after the motion creates contrast.

## Starting Image

Black screen. Empty. Then "423%" slams in — the first frame of motion should be the number at ~60% scale, mid-animation, so even the static thumbnail has the number visible.

## Ending Image

"Is someone copying YOUR business right now?" — white text centered on black. Source citation bottom-right. No logo. No CTA button. Clean.

## Brand Integration

- **Logo placement:** None. Awareness phase — no dotPH branding.
- **CTA:** None on the creative. Caption carries the engagement prompt.
- **Source citation:** "Check Point, 2025" — small text, bottom-right corner, visible throughout Scene 4.

# CREATIVE DIRECTION: AD 3 — "Cabanas La Union"

## Creative Summary

A 20-second reveal video that starts beautiful and ends empty. Opens with an AI-generated beach paradise — the kind of scene that stops a tourism business owner mid-scroll. Then systematically dismantles it: the bookings, the payments, the arrival, the nothing. The emotional arc is aspiration to horror. The viewer should feel the gut-punch of "this could be MY business being faked."

Designed for La Union / North Luzon geo-targeting. The beach visual hooks tourism operators; the reveal makes it personal.

## Specifications

- **Duration:** 20 seconds
- **Aspect Ratio:** 4:5 (1080x1350) for feed (primary), 9:16 for Stories
- **Platform:** Facebook Feed
- **Production:** AI-generated scenic visuals + text overlay animation
- **Objective:** Engagement / Video Views

## Music & Sound

- **Track description:** Acoustic guitar intro (warm, travel-ad feeling) that cuts abruptly to silence at 0:08. Then low drone/ambient tension for the reveal.
- **Sound design:** Gentle wave sounds (0:00-0:08). Hard audio cut to silence (0:08). Low bass drone (0:08-0:16). Text slam impact sound on "The resort didn't exist" (0:16).
- **Music arc:** Warmth → abrupt silence → tension → impact → silence on end card

## Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: "The Paradise" (0:00–0:05)

- **Duration:** 5 seconds
- **Visual:** AI-generated tropical beach scene — turquoise water, coconut palms, golden hour light. Looks like a travel ad. Slow zoom-in (Ken Burns style). Text overlay fades in: "This resort in La Union had 8,000 likes."
- **Text/VO:** "This resort in La Union had 8,000 likes."
- **Transition:** Color starts desaturating in the last second — subtle, almost imperceptible.
- **Mood:** Aspirational. Beautiful. This is what you'd want your resort to look like.
- **Technical:** AI-generated beach environment (Veo or Kling for subtle water/palm movement). 2% zoom over 5 seconds. Text: white, drop shadow, lower third.

### Scene 2: "The Bookings" (0:05–0:08)

- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Visual:** Same beach, now noticeably desaturated. Overlay shifts to a booking/payment mockup — calendar UI, GCash payment screen. Text: "Guests booked. They paid through GCash."
- **Text/VO:** "Guests booked. They paid through GCash."
- **Transition:** Hard cut to black at 0:08. Music and ambient sound cut simultaneously.
- **Mood:** Routine transaction. Normal. Nothing suspicious.
- **Technical:** Semi-transparent UI mockup overlay on the beach visual. Desaturation filter increases from 20% to 50% over 3 seconds.

### Scene 3: "The Arrival" (0:08–0:12)

- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Visual:** Black screen, then fade-in to an empty landscape — dry land, no structure, maybe a dirt road. AI-generated barren terrain. Google Maps-style pin drops onto the empty location. Text slams in: "Then they showed up."
- **Text/VO:** "Then they showed up."
- **Transition:** Text holds 2 beats, then new text builds below.
- **Mood:** Dread. Emptiness. The contrast with Scene 1 is the whole point.
- **Technical:** Empty terrain environment (AI-generated). Map pin animation drops from above with bounce. Text slam with slight camera shake.

### Scene 4: "The Truth" (0:12–0:17)

- **Duration:** 5 seconds
- **Visual:** Black background. Text appears line by line, each with a beat:
  - "No building." (beat)
  - "No DTI registration." (beat)
  - "Photos stolen from a resort in Batangas." (beat)
- **Text/VO:** Three lines, staggered.
- **Transition:** All lines fade to 30% opacity. Final line appears at full brightness.
- **Mood:** Systematic dismantling. Each line is a nail.
- **Technical:** Each line fades in from bottom with 0.8 second interval. Red accent color on key words: "No," "stolen."

### Scene 5: "The End Card" (0:17–0:20)

- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Visual:** Dark background, softer tone. Text fades in: "8,000 people liked a place that was never real." Below in smaller text: "Source: Verafiles, 2024." No product branding.
- **Text/VO:** End card text.
- **Transition:** Holds to end.
- **Mood:** Quiet weight. Let it sit.
- **Technical:** Simple fade-in. No animation. Stillness.

## Starting Image

AI-generated tropical beach at golden hour. Text overlay: "This resort in La Union had 8,000 likes." The thumbnail should look like a travel ad — that's the hook.

## Ending Image

"8,000 people liked a place that was never real." Dark background. Source citation. No logo.

## Brand Integration

- **Logo placement:** None. Awareness phase.
- **CTA:** None on creative. Caption carries the message.

# CREATIVE DIRECTION: AD 4 — "52% of Filipinos"

## Creative Summary

A 15-second motion graphic built around one devastating number: 52%. The approach is data-as-narrative — the number is the story, and each supporting stat adds weight. The emotional arc moves from a single stat to a systemic picture to a personal question. Clean, clinical, authoritative — like a news infographic that escaped the broadcast.

This works because Filipino business owners processing English-language data stats feel the institutional authority. The clinical tone signals "this is serious research, not a sales pitch."

## Specifications

- **Duration:** 15 seconds
- **Aspect Ratio:** 1:1 (1080x1080) for feed, 4:5 (1080x1350) alternative
- **Platform:** Facebook Feed
- **Production:** AI-generated motion graphics + kinetic typography
- **Objective:** Engagement / Video Views

## Music & Sound

- **Track description:** Minimal. Single sustained piano note or pad. Almost silence — the data should speak.
- **Sound design:** Soft impact on "52%" reveal. Subtle tick/counting sound on comparative stats. No voiceover.
- **Music arc:** Near-silence → soft tone (0:00) → sustained (0:03-0:12) → fade (0:12-0:15)

## Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: "The Number" (0:00–0:04)

- **Visual:** Dark navy background. "52%" appears center-screen — large, bold, white. Below: "of Filipinos have been scammed at least once." Smaller text, clean sans-serif.

- **Mood:** Weight. This is a national problem.
- **Technical:** "52%" enters with a subtle count-up animation (0% → 52% in 1.5 seconds). Supporting text fades in after count completes.

### Scene 2: "The Context" (0:04–0:08)

# CREATIVE DIRECTION: AD 7 — "Prevention vs. Prosecution"

## Creative Summary

A 20-second animated split-screen that visually contrasts two paths: the painful prosecution route (left, dark/red) versus the simple prevention route (right, light/green). The left side fills with bureaucratic steps piling up; the right side shows a clean 3-step process. The emotional arc is exhaustion to relief.

This is the first ad in the campaign that introduces Verified by dotPH. The split-screen format makes the product's value immediately visual — no one needs to read the caption to understand the proposition.

## Specifications

- **Duration:** 20 seconds
- **Aspect Ratio:** 1:1 (1080x1080) for feed (split-screen works best square)
- **Platform:** Facebook Feed
- **Production:** Motion graphics + kinetic typography. No scenic visuals needed.
- **Objective:** Traffic — drive to landing page

## Music & Sound

- **Track description:** Two-part music. Left channel: bureaucratic, tense, dissonant. Right channel: clean, minimal, resolving chord. Music should feel like the visual — split.
- **Sound design:** Paper shuffling, stamp sounds, clock ticking on the left side (0:00-0:10). Clean digital chime on the right side reveals (0:10-0:17). Resolution tone on end card.
- **Music arc:** Tension (left) → relief (right) → resolution (CTA)

## Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: "The Split" (0:00–0:03)

- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Visual:** Screen splits vertically. Left side: dark red/maroon background. Header animates in: "PROSECUTION." Right side: light green/white background. Header animates in: "PREVENTION." Dividing line is sharp and visible.
- **Text/VO:** "PROSECUTION" (left) | "PREVENTION" (right)
- **Transition:** Headers set. Left side begins filling.
- **Mood:** Clear binary choice.
- **Technical:** Vertical split animation — line draws from top to bottom. Headers slide in from outside edges. Bold sans-serif, all caps.

### Scene 2: "The Prosecution Pain" (0:03–0:10)

- **Duration:** 7 seconds
- **Visual:** Left side only activates. Steps pile up from bottom, each appearing with a stamp/thud:
  1. "File with DTI"
  2. "Report to PNP-ACG"
  3. "Sworn statements"
  4. "Screenshots & evidence"
  5. "Months of waiting"
  6. "Page taken down..."
  7. "...scammer creates a new one."
  The steps pile up and start overlapping, visually overwhelming. Right side stays empty — just the "PREVENTION" header, waiting.
- **Text/VO:** Seven steps, stacked.
- **Transition:** Left side is now visually crowded and chaotic. Beat. Right side activates.
- **Mood:** Bureaucratic exhaustion. Futility.
- **Technical:** Each step enters from the bottom with a slight upward push. Font size decreases as steps pile up (space compression). Slight red glow/pulse on "scammer creates a new one." Clock tick sound effect speeds up.

### Scene 3: "The Prevention Path" (0:10–0:17)

- **Duration:** 7 seconds
- **Visual:** Right side activates. Three steps appear, clean and spaced:
  1. "Submit your ID and documents." (checkmark animates)
  2. "dotPH verifies you're real." (checkmark animates)
  3. "Customers check your badge before they pay." (checkmark animates)
  Each step gets a green checkmark that draws itself. The left side is still visible but dimmed — the contrast is the point.
- **Text/VO:** Three clean steps with checkmarks.
- **Transition:** Right side complete. Both sides visible. Fade to end card.
- **Mood:** Clean. Simple. Relief. "This is so much easier."
- **Technical:** Steps fade in one at a time (2 sec each). Checkmark draws with a satisfying line animation. Green accent color. The left side drops to 40% opacity when the right side activates.

### Scene 4: "End Card" (0:17–0:20)

- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Visual:** Full screen (split dissolves). Center text: "Prevention beats prosecution. Every time." Below: dotPH logo (small) + CTA button visual: "Learn How — It's Free."
- **Text/VO:** End card + CTA.
- **Transition:** Holds to end.
- **Mood:** Resolution. Confidence.
- **Technical:** Split dissolves outward from center line. End card fades in. dotPH logo bottom-center. CTA button styled to match Meta's ad CTA format.

## Starting Image

Screen split vertically — "PROSECUTION" (dark red, left) vs "PREVENTION" (light green, right). Even as a still thumbnail, the binary choice is clear.

## Ending Image

"Prevention beats prosecution. Every time." Full screen, dotPH logo, CTA button.

## Brand Integration

- **Logo placement:** End card only (Scene 4). Small, bottom-center. First appearance of dotPH branding in the campaign.

# CREATIVE DIRECTION: POST C9 — "#CheckBeforeYouPay" Campaign Launch

## Creative Summary

A 30-second video that opens with a rapid montage of fake pages — resort pages, online stores, food delivery pages — each getting stamped "FAKE" in rapid succession. The viewer's anxiety builds: everything looks real, everything is fake. Then the Verified by dotPH badge enters the frame. A click animation. The verification page appears. Details match. Relief. The behavior is taught in 10 seconds of the video: see badge → click → check → pay.

This is the most important piece of consumer creative in the campaign. It launches the #CheckBeforeYouPay behavior and must work as both a paid boost and an organic post.

## Specifications

- **Duration:** 30 seconds
- **Aspect Ratio:** 4:5 (1080x1350) for feed, 9:16 (1080x1920) for Reels/Stories
- **Platform:** Facebook Feed (primary), Reels, Stories, Instagram
- **Production:** Motion graphics + screen recording mockups + text animation
- **Objective:** Engagement → habit formation

## Music & Sound

- **Track description:** Two-part track. Part 1 (0:00-0:15): tense, percussive, building urgency — electronic beats with glitch elements. Part 2 (0:15-0:30): resolving, confident — clean electronic with a satisfying tonal shift.
- **Sound design:** Rapid "FAKE" stamp sounds (0:02-0:12). Whoosh on badge reveal (0:15). Click sound (0:17). Digital chime on "Match" confirmation (0:22). Text appears: subtle type sound.
- **Music arc:** Chaos/urgency → pause → resolution/confidence → steady confidence on CTA

## Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1: "The Flood of Fakes" (0:00–0:10)

- **Duration:** 10 seconds
- **Visual:** Rapid montage. Facebook page mockups flash on screen — a resort page, an online clothing store, a food delivery page, a travel agency page. Each looks professional and real. Each gets a red "FAKE" stamp slammed across it after 1 second of display. Pages cycle faster and faster — 4 pages in 8 seconds, then 3 more in 2 seconds. The screen fills with overlapping "FAKE" stamps.
- **Text/VO:** Text overlay during montage: "Fake pages look real." (0:02) "Fake GCash links look real." (0:05) "Fake websites look real." (0:08)
- **Transition:** Screen fills completely with red "FAKE" stamps, overwhelming. Beat of silence (0.5 sec).
- **Mood:** Overwhelming. Everything is fake. How do you trust anything?
- **Technical:** Page mockups: static AI-generated screenshots of Facebook business pages. "FAKE" stamp: red, slightly rotated, slight screen shake on each stamp. Speed increases — first page holds 2 sec, last pages hold 0.5 sec each. Use Remotion for the rapid sequencing and stamp timing.

### Scene 2: "The Badge" (0:10–0:15)

- **Duration:** 5 seconds
- **Visual:** The chaos clears. Clean white/light background. A website footer mockup fades in — clean, professional. In the footer: the Verified by dotPH badge, glowing subtly. An arrow or highlight draws attention to it. Text overlay: "Look for the badge."
- **Text/VO:** "Look for the badge."
- **Transition:** Cursor/finger mockup moves toward the badge.
- **Mood:** Calm. Focus. Here's the answer.
- **Technical:** Clean transition from chaos to order. Badge should be the exact visual that consumers will see on real websites. Subtle glow/pulse animation on the badge (0.5 sec loop). Arrow is minimal — simple line with arrowhead.

### Scene 3: "The Click" (0:15–0:18)

- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Visual:** Cursor clicks the badge. Screen transitions (smooth morph or page-turn animation) to a verification page mockup on dotPH. The page loads with the business's details visible: Facebook page URL, GCash number, website URL.
- **Text/VO:** "Click it." appears briefly. Then the verification page speaks for itself.
- **Transition:** Smooth morph from website footer to verification page.
- **Mood:** Discovery. "Oh, this is how it works."
- **Technical:** Click animation: cursor compresses badge slightly (click feedback). Morph transition: badge expands to fill screen, then resolves into the verification page layout.

### Scene 4: "The Check" (0:18–0:23)

- **Duration:** 5 seconds
- **Visual:** Split-screen or side-by-side comparison. Left: the seller's GCash number (from the Facebook page). Right: the GCash number listed on the verification page. A line draws between them. Green checkmark animates: "Match." Text below: "It's the real business."
- **Text/VO:** "Check the details." → "GCash number matches?" → "It's real."
- **Transition:** Checkmark holds. Then transition to CTA.
- **Mood:** Confirmation. Relief. Confidence.
- **Technical:** Side-by-side with connecting line animation. Numbers should be clearly visible at mobile size. Green checkmark draws with a satisfying completion animation. Match confirmation has a subtle celebration effect (particles or light burst).

### Scene 5: "The CTA" (0:23–0:27)

- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Visual:** Clean background. Text builds line by line:
